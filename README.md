# Constitutional Vital Signs: A White Paper on Legitimacy, Care, and Civic Consciousness

## What is this?
A bridge artifact in the Constitutional Physiology lane of the Structural Flow project.

This white paper offers a public-facing way to read legitimacy as something more concrete than belief, mood, or compliance. It treats legitimacy as a measurable equilibrium shaped by care, aligned civic consciousness, and the proportional operation of law.

## Why should I care?
This paper gives cold readers a practical entry point into the Constitutional Physiology lane.

It asks whether a republic can be read the way a physician reads a patient: through vital signs, stress responses, circulation problems, inflammation, memory, and recovery. If that works, then legitimacy becomes more observable, more discussable, and more usable in real civic life.

## What is the claim?
The paper argues that legitimacy can be modeled through constitutional vital signs.

Its core relationship is:

**Legitimacy = Care + Aligned Civic Consciousness**

To make that publicly legible, the paper uses a small reading apparatus:
- Signal Proximity Index (SPI)
- Evasion Density (ED)
- Pain Acknowledgment Clause (PAC)
- Judicial Learning Index (JLI)

These are used as a public-facing interpretive framework for reading judicial attention, alignment, evasion, and learning.

## What is this not?
This artifact is not:
- the flagship Constitutional Physiology artifact
- a claim of final social-scientific closure
- a replacement for constitutional law or political theory
- a pure tool artifact
- enacted policy or legislation

It is a bridge artifact: explanatory, public-facing, and designed to help readers enter the deeper CP lane.

## How was this done?
This white paper reads a cross-section of judicial opinions as a constitutional attention record.

It treats judicial language as a visible trace of perception, evasion, acknowledgment, reflection, and learning, then maps those traces onto a civic-physiology framework.
